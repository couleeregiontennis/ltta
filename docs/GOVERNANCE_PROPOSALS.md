# LTTA 2026 Governance & Election Proposals

Currently, the league coordinator is "elected" at the preseason meeting by acclamation. As the league grows, a more formal process may be necessary.

---

## Proposal 1: Formal Nomination Window
*   **The Rule:** Nominations for all committee roles (Coordinator, Teams, On-Site) must be submitted to the current Coordinator 14 days before the preseason meeting.
*   **The Ballot:** If multiple people are nominated for a role, a paper ballot is held at the meeting (one vote per team).
*   **Goal:** Gives people time to think about volunteering rather than being "put on the spot" at a pizza party.

## Proposal 2: Defined Term Lengths
*   **The Rule:** Committee members serve a **2-year term**.
*   **Staggering:** To ensure stability, the League Coordinator and Teams Coordinator elections are held in alternating years.
*   **Goal:** Prevents "burnout" and ensures the league always has at least one experienced person in a leadership role.

## Proposal 3: Role "Manuals"
*   **The Rule:** Every outgoing committee member must provide a "1-page hand-off" document for their successor (contacts, keys, budget notes).
*   **Goal:** Makes it less intimidating for new people to step up.

## Proposal 4: Privacy & Secret Ballot Protocol
*   **The Problem:** Public "show of hands" voting can be awkward and may prevent honest feedback if members are afraid of offending current leadership.
*   **The Solution:** All contested elections must be conducted via secret ballot.
*   **Methods:**
    1.  **Paper Ballots:** Slips of paper handed out at the meeting, folded, and collected.
    2.  **Digital Anonymous Form:** A simple Google Form sent only to captains, with the "collect email" feature turned off.
    3.  **Neutral Tally:** Votes are counted by a non-candidate (e.g., an On-Site Coordinator or a neutral captain) and the final count is announced, but individual votes are never shared.
*   **Goal:** Protect relationships and ensure the league's leadership is truly chosen by the majority without social pressure.
